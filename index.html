Synopsis

"EDJOP" is a biopic about Edgar Jopson, a student activist who lived in Martial Law-era Philippines. A young man born to privilege, Edjop woke up to the brutal inequalities in his country and embraced the revolution of the times. He led the First Quarter Storm student rallies, demanding the dictator Marcos Sr. step down, and ultimately joined the underground movement. Edjop, a crucial actor of the Martial Law era, became one of Marcos' Most Wanted men. He was killed by the military in 1982.

Elijah Canlas

as Edgar "Edjop" Jopson

Elijah Canlas is a Filipino actor and performer whose work spans film, television, music and theater. He began acting at age 14 with Sundalong Kanin, an independent entry to the Cinemalaya Philippine Independent Film Festival. Canlas' breakthrough performance came in Kalel, 15, earning him Best Actor honors at the 43rd Gawad Urian Awards, 68th FAMAS Awards, the 17th Asian Film Festival in Rome, and the Harlem International Film Festival, among others. In 2020, he was named "Actor of the Year" by CNN Philippines. His recent work includes ensemble and leading roles in both drama and genre projects, establishing him as one of the most dynamic actors of his generation in Philippine cinema.

Jodi Sta. Maria is a Filipino actress and producer celebrated for her versatility across film, television, and independent cinema. With a career spanning more than two decades, she has delivered acclaimed performances in a wide range of genres, from drama and romance to horror and television serials. Sta. Maria has received multiple nominations from the FAMAS and Gawad Urian Awards and won Best Drama Actress at the Asian Academy Creative Awards in 2022. She was also nominated for Best Performance by an Actress at the 44th International Emmy Awards for her television work. Dubbed by some as one of the Philippines' most compelling performers, she brings depth and presence to every role she undertakes.
